eKYC Platform
  • Designed the eKYC platform end-to-end from a blank slate, as the bank had no existing eKYC capability — covering the customer data model, eKYC transaction/audit logging, and the workflow needed to qualify customers to a given IAL (Identity Assurance Level).
  • Designed the face liveness and face recognition pipeline used to authenticate customers against the facial biometric data captured during their eKYC enrollment at IAL Level 2.3, enabling secure face-based re-authentication for subsequent transactions.
  • Acted as technical lead for the platform, directing a mixed team of junior and senior developers — breaking down the architecture into workable tasks, reviewing code, and unblocking the team to keep delivery on the agreed timeline.
  • Designed and built integrations spanning internal and external systems: Core Banking (customer/account data), Counter Service (third-party agent network), TRUE SMS Gateway (OTP/notification delivery), and DOPA (Department of Provincial Administration, for government ID verification).
  • Enabled eKYC enrollment across multiple channels: in-branch via Android Tablets (including designing and building the Tablet backend service that bridges the tablet app to the eKYC platform) and via Counter Service's backend-to-backend data exchange for off-branch enrollment.
  • Extended the platform's data and trust model so it could feed into and interoperate with the NDID platform, allowing identity data verified through eKYC to be reused for cross-bank NDID identification.
NDID Platform
  • Designed as a dedicated, standalone platform separated from eKYC, scoped specifically to NDID (National Digital ID) operations rather than being bundled into the eKYC system.
  • Designed the NDID node architecture (the blockchain component of the NDID network) — covering the deployment of Tendermint, the node's API layer, message queue, and the secure storage of cryptographic keys and certificates — engineered to fit the bank's AWS Cloud Services environment.
  • Designed an internal application layer in front of the raw NDID API so that internal client teams acting as data-source owners could integrate and consume NDID services without needing to understand blockchain internals or the underlying NDID protocol flows.
  • Designed and developed the RP (Relying Party), IdP (Private Identity Provider), and AS/DP (Data Provider) services to satisfy business-unit requirements, including transaction reporting across RP/IdP/AS roles and the fee calculation logic tied to NDID transactions.
OCR Service
  • Designed an in-house OCR service to replace the bank's previous vendor-supplied OCR solution, bringing the capability in-house for greater control and extensibility.
  • Delivered the first version focused on Thai national ID card OCR — extracting structured identity data from the card image and forwarding it downstream to the eKYC and other dependent systems.
  • Architected the service with extensibility in mind, so the same OCR pipeline could later be extended to read additional document and image types beyond the national ID card.
